# Season 7 was awesome, but...
{% include post_header.html %}

**Good things do not last long enough**. Our perception makes us believe that good and enjoyable times zoom by way faster than their not so enjoyable counterparts.

Game of Thrones Season 7 is already over and to me it seems it just started last week, but in reality, it started 6 weeks ago. Granted, compared to the years before, it was a short season with just 7 instead of the 10 episodes that were the norm from season one to six.

Still, we have seen lots of awesome things and the plot has been brought to its first climax, the war for the Iron Throne between the challenger from across the narrow sea, our well-known Targaryan princess and the wannabe Queen Cersei who still sits on the Iron Throne. Additionally, the ground work for the final conflict between the human race and the White Walker has been laid.

We have seen Dragons, fire, epic battles, and it would not be GoT if we had not seen a few characters, even some central and important ones, pass away.

Cersei has indeed suffered a lot over the last 6 episodes. Effectively, she lost everything: Her father, her sons, her daughter, even her unborn child and it appears, she now lost her beloved brother as well. On top of that, we still remember what happened to her during season five at the hands of the High Sparrow: She was humiliated like no other Queen before, but no other than Cersei would have taken it without losing a single nuance of her dignity and we know, what kind of vengeance she had in store for those who treated her badly. She is our beloved antagonist, **because** she is evil and corrupted, but she is also believable. Everything she does makes sense and she acts like a corrupt regent would do in the real world in order to protect and preserve her position of power.

The fact that she paid a huge price works well to make her even more believable. If she would still enjoy everything she had at the beginning of the show, that character would certainly be less authentic and probably also less realistic: You can't be an asshole of such dimensions without ever having to pay a price.
