# Oscar Night!
{% include defs.md %}
{% include post_header.html %}

It's Oscar Night and so far, it's going pretty much straightforward and thus a bit disappointing. It's 
not the nominees or the movies themselves, in fact, there is a lot of great stuff this year, it's just 
that everything goes as expected and according to the plan. No big surprises up until now (I'm writing 
this while the nominees for best original score are announced).
{:dc} 
 
Speaking of original scores - here is one that deserved the Award more than any other in the last 10 
years, but came out blank.
 
{% include youtube.html id='UDVtMYqUAyw' %}

Hans Zimmer, who wrote that magnificent piece of sound track for the movie 
[Interstellar](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Interstellar_%28film%29), is among the nominees for the 
2018 award today with his creation for 
[Dunkirk](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dunkirk_%282017_film%29), but the award went to the sound 
track for [The Shape of Water](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/The_Shape_of_Water_%28film%29). Again, no 
surprise. 
 
## The Big Winner

**The Shape of Water** won a total of four awards, including the two „big ones” (best picture and 
[best director](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Guillermo_del_Toro)), followed by Dunkirk with three. 
Blade Runner 2049, Coco, Darkest Hour and Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri won two awards 
each. One golden guy each was awarded to I, Tonya, Get Out, Call Me by Your Name, A Fantastic Woman, 
Icarus, Phantom Thread, Heaven Is a Traffic Jam on the 405, The Silent Child, and Dear Basketball.

Clearly, there is no mega winner this year and overall, we have fifteen movies with at least one 
award. That's a lot more than last year's nine awarded productions and a good sign for the high number 
of quality productions among the nominees. 

## This and that

The Ceremony went without any noteworthy event, host Jimmy Kimmel delivered a solid, but rather 
un-exciting performance. At the beginning, he promised a price for the winner who would deliver the 
shortest thanks speech. Later in the show, he came up with a big surprise for the visitors of a nearby 
cinema just across the street of the famous Dolby Theatre. Kimmel and a number of celebrities, including 
Gal Gadot, Emily Blunt, Armie Hammer and many others, headed over, gifted the movie-goers snacks and 
candies, announcing „You're live at the Oscars” in the process. Whether it was staged or not, nobody 
knows. 

Warren Beatty and Faye Dunaway got their second chance to announce the winner of the best movie price 
and this time, they got it right (we still remember last year's [epic 
mishap](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=WSCuGj0pIww), don't we?). 
 
Frances McDormand, who won the Oscar for best actress for her stellar performance in Three 
Billboards... delivered an emotional acceptance speech with a clear statement for gender equality and 
more inclusion in Hollywood.
