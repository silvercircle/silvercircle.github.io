Oscar Night!

Mar.  2018 Monday 05
Last modified: 2018-03-05 06:02:36 +0100
448 Words
06:02 | by Alex Vie in Entertainment
Reading time: approx. 1 minute(s).

It’s Oscar Night and so far, it’s going pretty much straightforward and thus a bit disappointing. It’s not the nominees or the movies themselves, in fact, there is a lot of great stuff this year, it’s just that everything goes as expected and according to the plan. No big surprises up until now (I’m writing this while the nominees for best original score are announced).

Speaking of original scores - here is one that deserved the Award more than any other in the last 10 years, but came out blank.

Hans Zimmer, who wrote that magnificent piece of sound track for the movie Interstellar, is among the nominees for the 2018 award today with his creation for Dunkirk, but the award went to the sound track for The Shape of Water. Again, no surprise.

The Big Winner

The Shape of Water won a total of four awards, including the two „big ones” (best picture and best director), followed by Dunkirk with three. Blade Runner 2049, Coco, Darkest Hour and Three Billboards Outside Ebbing, Missouri won two awards each. One golden guy each was awarded to I, Tonya, Get Out, Call Me by Your Name, A Fantastic Woman, Icarus, Phantom Thread, Heaven Is a Traffic Jam on the 405, The Silent Child, and Dear Basketball.

Clearly, there is no mega winner this year and overall, we have fifteen movies with at least one award. That’s a lot more than last year’s nine awarded productions and a good sign for the high number of quality productions among the nominees.

This and that

The Ceremony went without any noteworthy event, host Jimmy Kimmel delivered a solid, but rather un-exciting performance. At the beginning, he promised a price for the winner who would deliver the shortest thanks speech. Later in the show, he came up with a big surprise for the visitors of a nearby cinema just across the street of the famous Dolby Theatre. Kimmel and a number of celebrities, including Gal Gadot, Emily Blunt, Armie Hammer and many others, headed over, gifted the movie-goers snacks and candies, announcing „You’re live at the Oscars” in the process. Whether it was staged or not, nobody knows.

Warren Beatty and Faye Dunaway got their second chance to announce the winner of the best movie price and this time, they got it right (we still remember last year’s epic mishap, don’t we?).

Frances McDormand, who won the Oscar for best actress for her stellar performance in Three Billboards… delivered an emotional acceptance speech with a clear statement for gender equality and more inclusion in Hollywood.
