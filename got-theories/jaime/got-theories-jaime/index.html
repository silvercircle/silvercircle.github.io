Theories about Jaime

Last modified: 2017-09-17 19:48:45 +0200
Written by Alex Vie | Sun, 17. September 2017, 19:48

Jaime’s moral stance is far less fucked up than his sister’s. He is like a troubled Jedi Knight who has fallen to the dark side, but there is still hope; he is not completely lost.

Jaime Lannister is one of my favorite characters next to Arya, The Hound, Cersei and Brienne. This was not always the case, I remember disliking him a lot during the first season and thought he was boring and pointless.

During season one, he was the barely likable arrogant, self centered, snob-ish kingslayer who believed he was the greatest sword fighter of all time and crippled an innocent young boy, because he and his even more evil sister didn’t want to face consequences for their treacherous and incestious behavior.

The defining moment, Jaime loses his sword hand

His excessive amount of self-confidence quickly took a sharp decline when he fell prisoner to the Starks at the end of season 1, and was ultimately shattered in season 3, when his fate as best sword fighter in all of Westeros was sealed at the hands of Locke and Jaime lost his sword hand in an act of utter atrocity.

Since then, he changed, but his loyalty for his family and his beloved sister probably prevented him from breaking up with Cersei earlier, the hints were always there though. It started during the famous steam bath scene when he explained to Brienne, what made him murder King Aerys II and ended with the final confrontation with his sister in the seventh season’s final episode. In between, he often enough showed that his moral stance is not even remotely as fucked up than his sister’s. Some examples:

  • He saved Tyrion, because he believed he was innocent. He had to trust him though, because back in season 4, no proof of Tyrion’s innocence did exist.

  • In season 6, he showed generosity and that he can solve conflicts by negotiation without having to draw blood during his deeds in Riverrun.

  • In season 7, he was the one who convinced Cersei Olenna should have a peaceful death. It is questionable whether he had still done this, had he known about Olenna being the master mind behind Joffrey’s gruesome death.
